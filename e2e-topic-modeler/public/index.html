How It Works

Three stages, from raw text to named topics — no coding required.

1

Embed & Vectorize

Your documents are transformed into TF-IDF vectors that capture the semantic fingerprint of each text — all computed locally in your browser.

2

Cluster

K-means clustering with cosine similarity groups documents that share underlying topical structure. Choose your own k or let the tool auto-detect it.

3

Explain

Representative documents from each cluster are sent to Claude, which names and describes every topic — then classifies every document for you.

About E2E Topic Modeling

A research-grade methodology made accessible through the browser.

Embedding-to-Explanation Topic Modeling is a hybrid analytic approach in which documents are first mapped into semantic embedding space using TF-IDF vectorization to identify clusters of language that reflect underlying topical structure, and are then passed to a large language model to convert those statistically derived clusters into clear, human-readable explanations.

This tool is designed for:

  • Researchers analyzing qualitative text data
  • Analysts exploring survey responses, reviews, or reports
  • Anyone with a collection of text documents who needs to find patterns

All embedding and clustering runs entirely in your browser. Only the topic-naming and classification stages communicate with the Claude API through a secure proxy — your raw documents are processed locally.

CSV Format

id,text
1,"This is my first document about climate change."
2,"Machine learning is transforming healthcare."
3,"The housing market continues to fluctuate."
4,"New renewable energy policies were announced."
...

Your CSV must include an id column and a text column. Additional columns are preserved and included in the results download. The file should be UTF-8 encoded.
